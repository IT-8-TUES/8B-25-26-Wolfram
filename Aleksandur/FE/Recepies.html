Porcini Mushroom

Classic Porcini Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 200g dried porcini mushrooms
  • 300g Arborio rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 100ml white wine
  • 1L vegetable broth
  • 50g Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Soak porcini mushrooms in warm water for 20 minutes
  2. Strain and chop, reserve the soaking liquid
  3. Sauté onion in olive oil until soft
  4. Add rice and toast for 2 minutes
  5. Add wine and let it evaporate
  6. Gradually add warm broth, stirring constantly
  7. Add mushrooms and continue until rice is creamy
  8. Finish with Parmesan and serve

Shiitake Mushroom

Grilled Shiitake Steaks

Ingredients:

  • 4 large shiitake mushroom caps
  • 3 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Mix olive oil, balsamic, garlic, and thyme
  2. Coat mushroom caps with marinade
  3. Let sit for 15 minutes
  4. Grill for 4-5 minutes per side
  5. Serve hot with remaining sauce

Oyster Mushroom

Creamy Oyster Mushroom Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 400g oyster mushrooms, sliced
  • 400g pasta
  • 200ml heavy cream
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Fresh parsley
  • Lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta according to package directions
  2. Melt butter in a large pan
  3. Sauté mushrooms and garlic until golden
  4. Add cream and simmer for 10 minutes
  5. Toss with cooked pasta
  6. Garnish with parsley and lemon juice

Chanterelle

Chanterelle Garlic Sauté

Ingredients:

  • 300g chanterelle mushrooms
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. Brush chanterelles clean and trim stems
  2. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat
  3. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds
  4. Toss in mushrooms and thyme, sauté until golden
  5. Season with salt and pepper
  6. Finish with parsley and serve warm

Fly Agaric

Fly Agaric Information

Warning: Fly Agaric is toxic and should not be eaten raw. Use only in traditional preparation where toxins are neutralized by proper drying or boiling, and only under expert guidance.

Traditional use: Historically, prepared fly agaric has been used in small doses in northern traditions after careful leaching. It is not recommended for casual cooking.

Morel

Butter-Braised Morels

Ingredients:

  • 250g fresh morel mushrooms
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 shallot, thinly sliced
  • 100ml dry white wine
  • Salt and pepper
  • Chopped chives

Instructions:

  1. Rinse morels quickly and pat dry
  2. Melt butter in a skillet and sauté shallot until translucent
  3. Add morels and cook for 5 minutes
  4. Pour in wine and reduce until syrupy
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and chives
  6. Serve on crusty bread or with grilled meat

Lion's Mane

Crispy Lion's Mane 'Steak'

Ingredients:

  • 1 large lion's mane mushroom
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Ground black pepper
  • Fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. Trim and slice the mushroom into thick pieces
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add garlic
  3. Place mushroom pieces in the pan and sear until golden
  4. Brush with soy sauce and pepper, cook until crisp
  5. Sprinkle with parsley and serve immediately

Truffle (Black)

Black Truffle Scrambled Eggs

Ingredients:

  • 4 eggs
  • 30g butter
  • 20g black truffle, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp crème fraîche
  • Salt and pepper
  • Chopped chives

Instructions:

  1. Whisk eggs with crème fraîche, salt, and pepper
  2. Melt butter in a nonstick pan over low heat
  3. Pour eggs into the pan and stir gently until curds form
  4. Remove from heat while still slightly soft
  5. Top with sliced truffle and chives
  6. Serve on toasted brioche or sourdough

Matsutake

Matsutake Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Japanese rice
  • 300g matsutake mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 cups dashi broth
  • Green onions

Instructions:

  1. Rinse rice until water runs clear
  2. Combine rice, broth, soy sauce, mirin, and salt in a pot
  3. Add sliced matsutake on top
  4. Bring to boil, then simmer covered for 15 minutes
  5. Turn off heat and let rest for 10 minutes
  6. Fluff rice, garnish with green onions, and serve

Reishi

Reishi Mushroom Tea

Ingredients:

  • 10g dried reishi slices
  • 1L water
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 slice ginger
  • Honey to taste

Instructions:

  1. Simmer reishi, cinnamon, and ginger in water for 30 minutes
  2. Strain the tea into cups
  3. Sweeten with honey if desired
  4. Drink warm as a gentle wellness beverage
